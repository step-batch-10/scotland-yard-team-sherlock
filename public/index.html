Scotland Yard

The Premise

Mr. X, a notorious criminal, is on the run through the city. Scotland Yard's finest detectives are in pursuit! To evade capture, Mr. X must move strategically and conceal his movements. The detectives must work together, using their wits and available information, to track him down. One player assumes the role of Mr. X, while the others play as detectives. The chase unfolds across the city, with players utilizing various modes of transportation: taxi, bus, and underground.

The Goal

If you are Mr. X: Your objective is to elude capture for as long as possible. You win if the detectives run out of transportation tickets.

If you are a Detective: Your goal is to apprehend Mr. X by landing on the same location he occupies.

Gameplay

Movement

All players travel between numbered locations within the city using taxi, bus, or underground routes.

  • Each move requires a specific transportation ticket.
  • Routes are color-coded: #63a4ff for taxi, green for bus, and red for the underground.
  • Locations indicate which transportation methods are available.
  • All locations allow taxi travel. Some locations also allow bus or underground travel.
  • Players must move on their turn.
  • A player cannot move to a location already occupied by another player.

Turns

  • Play proceeds in a clockwise direction, with Mr. X taking the first turn.
  • Detectives move all their assigned pieces (if any) before the turn passes.

Detective's Turn:

  • Spend the appropriate transportation ticket.
  • Move your pawn.
  • Detectives are encouraged to communicate and strategize.

Mr. X's Turn:

  • Mr. X's movements are hidden from the detectives.
  • Mr. X secretly selects his destination and the transportation used.
  • Mr. X's transportation choices provide clues to the detectives.
  • Mr. X's starting location is not recorded. Subsequent moves are recorded in sequence.
  • Mr. X's record of moves must be visible to the detectives.

Mr. X's Special Abilities

  • Surfacing: Mr. X must periodically reveal his location to the detectives.
    • The first reveal is after his 3rd move, and then after moves 8, 13, 18, and 24.
    • Mr. X's location is revealed on the game board.
  • Double Moves: Mr. X can use a limited number of special moves to travel to two locations in a single turn.
    • Both locations and transportation types are recorded.
  • Black Tickets: Mr. X has a limited number of special tickets that can be used to conceal the mode of transportation.
    • Black tickets can also be used for boat travel.
    • Mr. X can travel along the river using black tickets.

Game End

The game concludes in one of the following ways:

  • A detective lands on Mr. X's location, capturing him. The detectives win!
  • The detectives are unable to move due to a lack of fare tickets. Mr. X wins!
  • Mr. X successfully makes 24 moves without being captured. Mr. X wins!
  • The detectives are unable to move because they lack a particular type of ticket needed to move. Mr. X wins!

Strategic Tips

  • Detectives should start by positioning themselves to maximize mobility.
  • Mr. X should choose surfacing locations carefully.
  • Mr. X should use black tickets wisely, especially when detectives are closing in.
  • Players should pay close attention to the board layout and transportation options.

Advanced Rules

  • Mr. X can voluntarily surface at any time, receiving a ticket from each detective when he does so.
  • An optional rule prevents Mr. X from moving through a location occupied by a detective.
